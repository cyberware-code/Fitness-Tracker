Interval Progression Session

Goal: Gradually build endurance and efficiency at 6:00/km to complete a sub-30 5K.

Frequency: 1x per week as your main interval session.

Progression: Longer intervals, reduced recovery, and occasional speed work.

Full Session Breakdown

Week Reps Duration per Rep Pace (min/km) Treadmill Speed (kph)
Week 1 4 reps 6 min 6:00/km 10.0 kph
Week 2 3 reps 8 min 6:00/km 10.0 kph
Week 3 10 reps 400m 5:30-5:45/km 10.5-11.0 kph
Week 4 2 reps 10 min 6:00/km 10.0 kph
Week 5 2 reps + 1 push 10 min + 5 min 6:00/km 10.0 kph
Week 6 12 reps 400m 5:30-5:40/km 10.8-11.2 kph
Week 7 3K Continuous + 1 x 8 min 3K + 8 min 6:00/km 10.0 kph
Week 8 5K Continuous Run Full 5K 6:00/km 10.0 kph

How This Works

  • Weeks 1, 2, 4, 5, 7: Main interval progression (gradual increase in duration)
  • Weeks 3 & 6: 400m speed sessions improve leg turnover and neuromuscular coordination.
  • Week 8: Final 5K continuous run attempt at 6:00/km to test race readiness.
  • Obsidian links: Ensure each session is easy to reference and track performance.

Overall Strategy

This structured approach keeps you on track for a sub-30 5K while preventing burnout and promoting consistent improvement.
